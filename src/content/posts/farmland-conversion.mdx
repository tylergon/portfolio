For a research proposal, I shifted focus to explore how I could quantify human geography with spatial modelling. The South Coast of British Columbia is home to a vast portion of the province's population, but the increasing population pressures and land cost threaten to push urban expansion into the provinces prime agricultural land. The province established the Agricultural Land Reserve (ALR) in the 20th century, which should be protecting agricultural lands under the constant pressures of urban expansion.

To explore the progression of this relationship, my proposed study asks how population growth has affected the total area of farmland from 1981 to 2021.
